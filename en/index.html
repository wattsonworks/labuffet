The breakfast

A morning that begins with warm bread and ends with coffee in the courtyard.

La Buffet is The Way Inn's breakfast — an open, generous, home-cooked table laid out for hotel guests in the big stone dining room and the cobblestone courtyard. Breads baked here that same morning, Mediterranean salads and spreads, local cheeses, eggs, seasonal fruit, and unhurried coffee.

The setting

A 250-year-old stone house in the Artists' Quarter.

La Buffet is the breakfast served at The Way Inn — a small boutique hotel inside an old stone house, founded by chef Rony BarEl and his wife Genine. About ten suites around sheltered courtyards, rooftop sunset patios, a Turkish hammam, and the air of old Tzfat in every wall.

In the morning, the big stone room fills with Galilee light and the scent of warm bread. The hotel's napkins are embroidered with a Tree-of-Life mandala — the same mandala set into the courtyard wall. This is not a hotel restaurant. It is BarEl's home, generous when you stay over.

"Not just visiting Tzfat — inhabiting it."
